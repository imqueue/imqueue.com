Licensing

Do you need a commercial license for @imqueue?

@imqueue is dual-licensed. Everything is free and open under GPL-3.0 on imqueue.org. A commercial license lets you ship the same packages inside a closed-source product without the GPL's source-release obligation — plus support on top.

When does the GPL-3.0 obligation apply?

The copyleft trigger is distribution. Here's the plain-language version — use it to figure out which side of the line you're on.

Shipping a closed-source product

You distribute an app, a device, or on-prem software built on @imqueue and don't want to release its source. A commercial license is the clean path.

Internal-only tools

The software runs inside your organization and isn't distributed outside it. The GPL's distribution trigger generally isn't hit — though many teams still license for support and indemnification.

A SaaS / hosted service

GPL-3.0 is not the AGPL: running @imqueue behind a network service you host usually isn't "conveying," so the source-release obligation generally isn't triggered by SaaS use alone.

Licensing questions

Is @imqueue really free?

Yes. The full framework is open source under GPL-3.0 on imqueue.org — free to use, modify, and distribute under the GPL's terms. The commercial license is only needed when those terms don't fit your product.

Can I use the GPL-3.0 version in a commercial project?

Yes — GPL-3.0 permits commercial use. The obligation is about source disclosure on distribution, not about charging money. If you distribute a product built on it, the GPL asks you to release that product's source; a commercial license removes that requirement.

Does running @imqueue in a SaaS trigger the GPL?

Generally no. GPL-3.0 is not the AGPL — providing access to a hosted service isn't "conveying" the software, so SaaS use alone typically doesn't trigger the source-release obligation. A commercial license is still available if you want support or indemnification.

Is the commercial code different from the open-source code?

No — the exact same @imqueue/* packages and APIs. A commercial license changes the terms you use them under, not the software. There's no separate fork to install or migrate to.
